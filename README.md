## üíª Projects and Learnings üíª

#### [Numbers Game](https://github.com/helenaroughton/numbers-game)
A number learning game for my young niece which has one learning level built with JavaScript.
My first solo project which I'm still developing but have already experienced two great learning and problem solving moments.\
\
**1. Code Structure:**\
Importance of finding a balance between "Separation of Concerns" and "Bundling". I had over separated components which made data management difficult. I struggled to connect the logic with the data and after comparing against other projects and guidance from Claude I realised I needed to adopt a more bundled approach to keep data and logic in the same component.\
\
**2. Product Development:**\
Vital to have regular check ins with the end user or customer of the product to ensure it remains fit for purpose and usable. My first iteration was too complex for the target user and after the second iteration, extra feedback led to valuable additions such as audio prompts and naming of animals for further development. I also learned not to over style too early and create significant re-work after user feedback. 


#### [Meet Me in the Middle](https://github.com/helenaroughton/meet-me) 
An app where the user can find the perfect place (park, cafe, bar, restaurant) to meet their friend in the middle of where they both live.\
\
I have had some valuable learnings in the planning stages:\
\
**1. Simple MVP vs Product Goal**\
I made a decision to focus on getting the simplest version to market to test the core concept as soon as possible over widening the practical use cases of the app before launching. This was a hard decision for me and I originally had many extra use cases and features I planned to include in the first MVP but after working through my project plan I realised I should focus on developing the simplest version first and have phases that follow.
